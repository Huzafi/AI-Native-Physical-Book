# Section Outline

## Part I: Foundations of Intelligence

### Chapter 1: Perception - How Intelligence Gathers Information
- Core concept: Perception as the foundation of intelligent behavior
- Comparison: Human vs. AI perception mechanisms
- Key elements: Multi-sensory integration, pattern recognition, feature extraction
- Applications: Computer vision, sensory processing systems

### Chapter 2: Reasoning - How Intelligence Processes Information
- Core concept: Reasoning as the bridge between perception and action
- Comparison: Human intuitive vs. analytical reasoning vs. AI logical/probabilistic reasoning
- Key elements: Inference, problem-solving, causal understanding
- Applications: Decision-making systems, logical inference engines

### Chapter 3: Physical AI & Humanoids - Intelligence in the Physical World
- Core concept: Embodied intelligence that interacts with the physical environment
- Comparison: Human physical interaction vs. robotic systems and humanoids
- Key elements: Sensorimotor integration, human-robot interaction, safety and ethics
- Applications: Assistive robotics, manufacturing, healthcare, service industries
